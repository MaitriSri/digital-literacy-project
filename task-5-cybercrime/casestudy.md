# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, targeting users through social engineering techniques. In this type of fraud, attackers trick victims into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or approving fraudulent payment requests.

A common scenario involves a fraudster posing as a bank representative or customer support agent. The victim receives a call or message claiming there is an issue with their bank account or that they are eligible for a reward. The attacker then sends a "collect request" on a UPI app like Google Pay or PhonePe. The victim is instructed to enter their UPI PIN to "receive" money, but in reality, this action authorizes a payment to the fraudster.

Another method includes fake QR codes or phishing links that redirect users to malicious websites designed to steal credentials.

Students and young adults are often targeted due to their frequent use of digital payment apps and lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, data theft, and mental stress.

UPI fraud highlights the importance of awareness and cautious behavior while performing digital transactions.
